Spatial Cues Enhanced Multi-Channel Band-Split RNN for Regional Sound Extraction

Xiang Zhou1, Yichen Yang1,2, and Wen Zhang1
1Center of Intelligent Acoustics and Immersive Communications, School of Artificial Intelligence, Northwestern Polytechnical University, Xi'an, Shaanxi, China
2Graduate School of Information, Production and Systems, Waseda University, Kitakyushu, Japan


Abstract: Regional sound extraction, which aims to isolate all active sound sources within a specific spatial region defined by direction and distance, is a critical technology with numerous methods developed for the task. However, the performance of these methods often deteriorates when interference sources are located in close spatial proximity to the target region. To overcome this limitation, we propose a spatially informed multi-channel band-split recurrent neural network (BSRNN) system. The system incorporates two novel modules for fine-grained utilization of spatial cues: a target directions embedding generator to represent directional information and a distance and reverberation time embedding generator to capture distance-related features and room reverberation features. We perform extensive evaluations on both simulated and real-world datasets. The results show that the proposed method substantially outperforms baseline approaches in multiple metrics. Notably, it achieves high spatial resolution, sustaining strong separation performance even when sources are separated by very small angles and distances, demonstrating its effectiveness in challenging scenarios with closely spaced talkers.
